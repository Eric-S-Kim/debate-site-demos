Caveats

Do not let the dashboard become theater.

The plan only works if the metrics steer real behavior: sleep, lifting, cardio, recovery, and honest bottleneck identification.

01 · Bottleneck

Your actual WHOOP Healthspan screen is paramount: do not guess your bottleneck. The input adding the most years is your primary target.

02 · Sleep disruptors

Late-night coding and alcohol consumption are significant, often underestimated, levers that can derail sleep consistency and recovery, directly impacting WHOOP metrics.

03 · Steering wheel

Differentiate WHOOP Age, the long-term score, from Pace of Aging, the short-term steering wheel, for daily decision-making.

04 · Tissue timing

Ramp up new lifting loads conservatively; connective tissue adapts slower than muscle at 40+, risking injury.

05 · Interference

Manage interference: do not stack multiple high-strain activities, such as heavy legs, hard HIIT, and intense dance, on low-recovery days.

Action plan

Six moves for the next training block.

Start by finding the actual WHOOP bottleneck, then run a recomposition system that trains strength, VO2max, sleep consistency, and visible physique progress together.

Today: Open WHOOP Healthspan

Identify your 1-2 biggest bottlenecks. Note your current WHOOP Age, Pace of Aging, and which specific inputs, such as VO2max, lean mass, and sleep consistency, are contributing most to your WHOOP Age.

Weekly Training Template

Implement 3-4 progressive resistance training sessions per week, focused on compound lifts, 2 Zone 2 cardio sessions of 30-45 minutes, and 1 structured Zone 4-5 VO2max session per week, such as 4x4 intervals, gradually increasing intensity. Maintain 1-2 joyful movement sessions, dance or yoga, as desired.

Nutrition Foundation

Target 1.6-2.2 g/kg protein, 125-155g for ~170lb, distributed across meals. Start at maintenance calories or a mild 200-300 calorie deficit for recomposition. Prioritize whole, nutrient-dense foods.

Protect Sleep

Establish a non-negotiable bedtime, aiming for 7-9 hours of quality sleep. Implement a hard stop on late-night screens and coding.

Use WHOOP as Your Guardrail

Use your daily WHOOP recovery score as your guide: Green means push; Yellow means moderate; Red means active recovery and mobility. Focus on weekly trends for Pace of Aging, not daily fluctuations.

Track Progress Weekly

Monitor weekly waist measurements, bi-weekly progress photos with consistent lighting and pose, a gym log for lift progression, and key WHOOP Healthspan metrics: VO2max, RHR, sleep consistency, HR zone time, and strength time.
