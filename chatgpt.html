Secure Must-Have Tickets Now, Strategize Resale Later

For high-demand LA28 events, primary sales offer the best chance for face value and authenticity; waiting, as Gemini put it, is a high-stakes gamble you're likely to lose.

Why this wins

Primary sales beat hope.

The logic is blunt: lock down the scarce, emotional, high-demand sessions through official channels. Treat resale as optionality, not salvation.

1

High Demand, Limited Inventory

LA28 is a major event with global demand. Primary sales are your best opportunity to secure tickets for iconic events, medal sessions, or highly popular sports before inventory becomes scarce and prices escalate.

2

Avoid Early Fraud Risk

LA28 explicitly states it cannot verify resale tickets before its official program launches in 2027. Purchasing from unofficial channels now exposes you to significant risk of scams, counterfeit tickets, and inflated prices.

3

Resale Favors Sellers

For desirable events, the secondary market, even official verified resale, typically rewards sellers. Do not expect significant discounts; instead, anticipate higher prices for popular sessions if you wait.

Best to worst.

Buy the dream through primary sales. Buy the extras later. Never gamble with unofficial early resale.

The clean rule: secure the scarce.

Do not ask resale to solve your must-have problem. Resale is for upgrades, schedule gaps, and nice-to-have sessions once travel plans are firmer.

Pitfalls

Don’t do these.

Do not wait for resale on your true 'must-see' events; this is a gamble you will likely lose.
Do not assume 'resale' means 'discount'; for popular events, it often means higher prices.
Do not buy from unofficial sources or street scalpers before LA28's official resale program (2027).
Do not blow your entire budget on one prestige session; diversify your Olympic experience.
Do not ignore venue geography; LA28 events are spread out, so plan accordingly to minimize travel friction.

What would change the answer?

If LA28 announces in 2027 that verified resale is capped near face value and has abundant supply for all event types, this would make waiting much more attractive for flexible events.

Action plan

Five moves. No drama.

The operational play: get eligible, prioritize ruthlessly, set ceilings, buy A-list first, preserve resale budget.

01

Immediately register for LA28 Drop 2 (deadline July 22, 2026) and all official ticketing alerts.

02

Create a prioritized list of 'must-have' (A-list) and 'nice-to-have' (B-list) events.

03

Before your purchase window, set strict price ceilings for your A-list events.

04

During your purchase window, buy only your A-list tickets first, then add B-list if prices are sane.

05

Save 30-50% of your total budget for official, verified resale opportunities beginning in 2027 to fill gaps or upgrade.
