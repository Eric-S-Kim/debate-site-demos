The field,
ranked.

The best option is not the most intense immersion. It is the one that creates a hard output gate while still letting GStack touch the rest of real life lightly enough to learn what deserves to be ported.

1
DO FIRST

Anchored Extraction Week

Ship one key deliverable using deep GStack judgment, while testing a thin operating layer across the rest of life. Best default for balancing output & evaluation.

2
RUNNER-UP

Pure Output Sprint

Focus solely on shipping one high-stakes deliverable with GStack. Best if an urgent deadline demands it, but misses broader life integration.

3

Pre-Architected Full

Extensive multi-week pre-setup to migrate and broadly integrate GStack. This is a high-risk strategy, prone to “productive procrastination” as setup becomes the goal.

4
AVOID

Chaos / Feature Safari

Unbounded exploration, trying everything everywhere. All advisors rejected this approach as leading to tool thrashing and no concrete output.

Rules that
keep it honest.

This is an extraction test, not a migration fantasy. Keep it bounded, physical, private where needed, and judged by output rather than novelty.

Caveats

  • 1Define “shipped” concretely for your anchor deliverable, e.g., “sent to coauthors,” not just “progress made”.
  • 2Prioritize GStack as a judgment system — reframing and adversarial review — over a deep operations system for this week.
  • 3Do not let GStack increase screen time, reduce movement, or worsen sleep; it should make you less fragmented.
  • 4Clarify privacy and account boundaries: what data, including academic or health data, can freely enter GStack, what must be redacted, or stays local.
  • 5This is primarily an extraction test — what to port — not a full migration test.
  • 6GStack’s utility is mainly in its four judgment-shaped gears: /office-hours, /plan-ceo-review, /codex, and /investigate, not its code-specific ones.

Falsification

If your manuscript submission count actually rose in the six months after building your existing stack, the goal-displacement worry is falsified, making the output-gate potentially unnecessary overhead.

The week
operating system.

A practical seven-part protocol: tight setup, one external ship target, daily judgment blocks, thin whole-life capture, a mid-week failure drill, and a Friday decision brief.

Action Plan Output-gated · broad testing · no new infrastructure

Ship by Friday.

The week is won only if a real deliverable leaves your private workspace and enters the world.

Day 0
2–3 hrs max
Pick one most avoided, consequential deliverable, e.g., Add Health R&R. Define “shipped” as a concrete, external output by Friday 5 pm. Freeze your tech stack — no new tools or refactors. Create a Week Log: anchor progress, daily briefs, keep/adapt/discard.
Configure
Devices
Uninstall or hide competing apps, set GStack as defaults on phone and computer, and clarify account strategy for personal and work use.
Daily Brief
20 min AM
Frame today’s anchor move, identify emails/calendar risks, note life constraints, define one daily output, and protect energy.
Deep Work
90–150 min
Use GStack’s judgment gears — /office-hours, /plan-ceo-review, /codex, /investigate — exclusively on the anchor deliverable: frame, produce, adversarial review, cut, commit.
Whole-Life
Thin Layer
All other emails, tasks, life admin, and mobile capture enter GStack only via lightweight capture-triage-prepare-close loops. No deep redesign.
Mid-Week
Failure Drill
Deliberately test GStack on messy cases; classify cause of failure — retrieval, reasoning, prompt, context — and define fix or kill.
Friday
Ship & Decide
Submit or send the anchor deliverable. Create a one-page decision brief: 3–6 GStack patterns to keep, ideas to adapt, what to discard, and new rules/boundaries.
