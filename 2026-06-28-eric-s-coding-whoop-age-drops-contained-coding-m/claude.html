Why this wins

Three forces that decided it.

01

Coding Curfew is Non-Negotiable

All advisors agree edgeless coding — especially AI agent loops — must be contained by hard start/stop times and a strict evening curfew to protect sleep and recovery, as Claude emphasized for stopping evening sprawl.

02

Movement is Infrastructure

Physical activity must be a proactive, structural component of the day — not something earned after coding. As ChatGPT put it: movement is not dessert, it is infrastructure for health and cognitive function.

03

Recovery Gates Intensity

A high movement floor is essential, but "max movement" without recovery is counterproductive. Activity — though ambitious — must be recovery-gated by WHOOP data to optimize adaptation and avoid overtraining; a crucial refinement by Claude and Gemini.

The Protocol

Seven moves, starting tonight.

1

Set a hard coding curfewTonight, define a non-negotiable "no serious coding after [e.g., 7 PM]" rule. Serious = debugging, new features, agent runs; allowed = notes, light planning.

2

Schedule one primary coding blockDefault to 90–150 minutes daily. Before starting, write down what you're shipping, the stop condition, and what is explicitly out of scope for that session.

3

Close the agent loopAt the end of the block, explicitly stop all agents, close related terminals/tabs, write a clear handoff/next action, and physically leave your workstation.

4

Establish a movement floorTarget 10,000+ steps/day or 70,000–90,000/week. Leverage daily Cupcake walks and scheduled dance/Ballet BC sessions as non-negotiable movement blocks.

5

Add structured cardio & strengthInclude 4–5 meaningful movement days weekly (2 harder cardio/strain, 2–3 Zone 2/steady) and 2 minimum-dose strength sessions of 35–45 minutes each.

6

Recovery-gate intensityAdjust workout intensity and volume to your WHOOP recovery score. On yellow/red days, swap high-intensity for restorative movement (Zone 2 walk, mobility) — but never skip movement entirely.

7

Review the weekly scoreboardEvery Sunday, review contained coding sessions, shipped artifacts, curfew adherence, weekly steps, structured cardio/strength, sleep duration/consistency, and recovery metrics (RHR, HRV).

Watch-outs

Caveats worth holding.

  • AI agent loops are cognitive open loops; ensure they're explicitly closed when your coding block ends.
  • Don't use a "yellow recovery" day as an excuse to skip movement entirely; swap intensity (hard dance → Zone-2 walk) instead, as Claude advised.
  • WHOOP Age is a longer-term measure; focus on consistent input behaviors (sleep, HR-zone time, strength time, steps, VO₂ max, RHR) over daily fluctuations.
  • Integrate nutrition and hydration as foundational pillars for recovery — a blind spot for all advisors.
  • Consider the deeper "why" of your coding (building durable systems) and movement (walks with Cupcake) for sustained motivation, not just metrics.
